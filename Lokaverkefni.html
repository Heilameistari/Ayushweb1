Lokaverkefni

Í lokaverkefninu í þessum áfanga var hópverkefni. Í hópnum okkar var ég, Leo og Halldór og við ákvöðum að búa til skóhillu. Ofan á skóhillunni ætlum við að fræsa einhverja mynd sem er í þema við verkefnið.

Hluti 1: Ayush

Hlutverkið mitt í verkefninu var að teikna skóhilluna frá grunni í Fusion 360. Hlutverk Halldórs var að velja myndina sem verður fræst ofan á skóhilluna. Hlutverk Leo var að setja skóhilluna saman og að teikna hlífina undir lappirnar svo að hillan renni ekki mikið.

Ég byrjaði með að finna mér smá innblástur um hvernig skóhillu mig langaði að búa til. Það voru nokkur hönnun sem mig leist mikið á sem hægt er að sjá hér fyrir neðan.

Hluti 3: Leonardo

Introduction

In this project, we will design and produce an object using a CNC milling machine (Shopbot). We were a group of three people: Ayush-Tseren, Halldór, and myself. Ayush will design the body, Halldór will create the design for the picture, and I will design the cover for the shoe rack.

Process

During the cutting process, our teacher, Hafliði, provided guidance and assistance. He helped us set up the Shopbot, ensuring that all parameters were correctly configured for the cutting procedure.

After cutting our design, I proceeded with the post-processing phase, which involved removing and trimming the excess wood. This was followed by sanding to achieve a smooth finish.

The next day, we assembled the pieces together using screws at the appropriate positions.

After this, I began designing the feet covers for the shoe rack.

While the process itself was straightforward, its underlying purpose was more complex. We recognized the potential for the wood to damage the parquet flooring. To address this issue, I designed covers for the shoe rack feet, which were intended to be 3D printed. Initially, we planned to use UTP plastic, a moldable material that seemed suitable for the task. However, after several tests, we found that the 3D printer was unable to print with this material, despite configuring the printer settings correctly.

Consequently, we opted to use PLA plastic, which proved to be a successful alternative.

This is the final product.
